Homogeneous Solution

The state-space model denoted as \[ \dot x=Ax+Bu \\ y=Cx+Du \] First we want to solve \[ \dot x=Ax \\ x=e^{A(t-t_0)}x_0 \\ t_0=0 \\ x=e^{At}x(0) \] ## Properties of \(e^{At}\)

  1. \(\frac{d}{dt}e^{At}=Ae^{At}=e^{At}A\)
  2. \(e^{At}=\mathcal{L}^{-1}[(sI-A)^{-1}]\)
  3. \(e^{At_1}e^{At_1}=e^{A(t_1+t_2)}\)
  4. \([e^{At}]^T=e^{A^Tt}\)
  5. \(e^{At}e^{Bt}=e^{(A+B)t}\) only and only if AB=BA
  6. \(e^{At}=\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^\infty \frac{A^kt^k}{k!}\)

How to Solve \(e^{At}\)

  1. Series Expansion

\[ e^{At}=I+At+\frac{1}{2!}A^2t^2+\dotsm+\frac{1}{n!}A^nt^n+\dotsm \]

  1. Using Inverse Laplace Transform

    Example:

\[ A =\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\-2 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \]

\[ e^{At}=\mathcal{L}^{-1}[(sI-A)^{-1}] \\ (sI-A)= \begin{bmatrix} s & -1 \\ 2 & s+3 \end{bmatrix}\\ (sI-A)^{-1}=\frac{1}{s(s+3)+2} \begin{bmatrix} s+3 & 1 \\ -2 & s \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} \frac{s+3}{(s+1)(s+2)} & \frac{1}{(s+1)(s+2)} \\ \frac{-2}{(s+1)(s+2)} & \frac{s}{(s+1)(s+2)} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{s+1}-\frac{1}{s+2} & \frac{1}{s+1}-\frac{1}{s+2} \\ \frac{-2}{s+1}-\frac{-2}{s+2} & \frac{-1}{s+1}-\frac{-2}{s+2} \end{bmatrix}\\ \mathcal{L}^{-1}[*^{-1}]= \begin{bmatrix} 2e^{-t}-e^{-2t} & e^{-t}-e^{-2t} \\ -2e^{-t}+2e^{-2t} & -e^{-t}+2e^{-2t} \end{bmatrix} \]

  1. Using Caley-Hamilton Theorem

Example \[ A =\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\-2 & -3 \end{bmatrix}\\ f(\lambda)=e^{\lambda t} \\ h(\lambda)=\beta_0+\beta_1\lambda \\ \lambda_1=-1 \quad \lambda_2=-2 \\ \begin{array}{} e^{-t}&=&\beta_0+\beta_1(-1) \\ e^{-2t}&=& \beta_0+\beta_1(-2) \\ \end{array}\\ \text{We can get the result:} \\ \beta_0=2e^{-t}-e^{-2t} \\ \beta_1=e^{-t}-e^{-2t} \\ \text{Then we have:} \\ h(\lambda)=2e^{-t}-e^{-2t}+(e^{-t}-e^{-2t})\lambda\\ f(A)=h(A)=(2e^{-t}-e^{-2t})I+(e^{-t}-e^{-2t})A = \begin{bmatrix} 2e^{-t}-e^{-2t} & e^{-t}-e^{-2t} \\ -2e^{-t}+2e^{-2t} & -e^{-t}+2e^{-2t} \end{bmatrix} \]

  1. Using Diagonalization

Full Solution

\[ x(t)=e^{At}x(0)+\int_0^te^{A(t-\tau)}Bu(\tau)d\tau \] Example: \[ \dot x=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -2 & 3 \end{bmatrix} x+\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} u \] Input is unit step.

We have get the solution in the previous \[ e^{At}=\begin{bmatrix} 2e^{-t}-e^{-2t} & e^{-t}-e^{-2t} \\ -2e^{-t}+2e^{-2t} & -e^{-t}+2e^{-2t} \end{bmatrix} \] Then \[ \int_0^te^{A(t-\tau)}Bd\tau=\int_0^t\begin{bmatrix} 2e^{-(t-\tau)}-e^{-2(t-\tau)} & e^{-(t-\tau)}-e^{-2(t-\tau)} \\ -2e^{-(t-\tau)}+2e^{-2(t-\tau)} & -e^{-(t-\tau)}+2e^{-2(t-\tau)} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}d\tau \\ =\int_0^t \begin{bmatrix} e^{-(t-\tau)}-e^{-2(t-\tau)} \\ -e^{-(t-\tau)}+2e^{-2(t-\tau)} \end{bmatrix}d\tau = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{2}-e^{-t}+\frac{1}{2}e^{-2t} \\ e^{-t}-e^{-2t} \end{bmatrix} \] ## Relationship Between T.F. Matrix and S.S. Equation

Example \[ \dot x=Ax+Bu\\ y=Cx \\ A= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -\frac{k}{m} & -\frac{b}{m} \end{bmatrix} \quad B=\begin{bmatrix} 0\\\frac{1}{m} \end{bmatrix}\quad C=[1 \quad 0] \] After Laplace Transform \[ sX(s)=AX(s)+BU(s) \\ X(s)=(sI-A)^{-1}BU(s) \\ Y(s)=C(sI-A)^{-1}BU(s) \\ G(s)=C(sI-A)^{-1}B \\ (sI-A)=\begin{bmatrix} s & -1\\\frac{k}{m} & s+\frac{b}{m} \end{bmatrix}\\ (sI-A)^{-1}=\begin{bmatrix} \frac{ms+b}{ms^2+bs+k} & \frac{m}{ms^2+bs+k} \\ \frac{-k}{ms^2+bs+k} & \frac{ms}{ms^2+bs+k} \end{bmatrix}\\ G(s)=\frac{1}{ms^2+bs+k} \] ## Modes of System

Consider \(\dot x=Ax\) \[ Q=[q_1 \quad q_2 \quad\dotsm\quad q_n] \] where \(q_i\) are the eigenvectors of A \[ Q^{-1}= \begin{bmatrix} p_1 \\ p_2 \\ \vdots \\ p_n \end{bmatrix} \] Where \(p_i\) is ith row vector of Q-1 .

Then we have \[ x(t)=\sum_{i=1}^ne^{\lambda_it}q_ip_ix_0 = \sum_{i=1}^ne^{\lambda_it}q_i\mu_i \] Where \(\mu_i=p_ix_0\)

The above shows that the solution is the sum of n modes.

Example \[ \dot x= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1\\ -4 & -5\\ \end{bmatrix}x\\ \] Eigenvalues are -1 and -4 \[ Q=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1\\ -1 & 4\\ \end{bmatrix} Q^{-1}= \frac{1}{3}\begin{bmatrix} 4 & 1\\ 1 & 1\\ \end{bmatrix}\\ p_1=[\frac{4}{3}\quad\frac{1}{3}]\\ p_2=[\frac{1}{3}\quad\frac{1}{3}]\\ x_1(t)=\frac{1}{3}[(4x_{10}+x_{20})e^{-t}-(x_{10}+x_{20})e^{-4t}]\\ x_2(t)=\frac{1}{3}[(-4x_{10}-x_{20})e^{-t}+(4x_{10}+4x_{20})e^{-4t}]\\ \]

LTV General Solution

\[ X=[x_1 \quad x_2 \quad \dotsm \quad x_n] \\ \dot{x}=Ax \\ \dot X(t)=A(t)X(t) \\ \phi(t,t_0)=X(t)X(t_0)^{-1} \\ x(t)=\phi(t,t_0)x(t_0) \]

With \(\varphi(t,t_0)\) , we can get the full solution \[ x(t)=\varphi(t,t_0)x(t_0)+\int_{t_0}^{t}\varphi(t,\tau)B(\tau)u(\tau)d\tau \] Example \[ \dot x(t)=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ t & 0 \end{bmatrix} x(t)\\ x_1(t)=x_1(0) \\ x_2(t)=0.5t^2x_1(0)+x_2(0)\\ x(0)=\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \rightarrow x(t)=\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0.5t^2 \end{bmatrix}\\ x(0)=\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \rightarrow x(t)=\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0.5t^2+1 \end{bmatrix}\\ X(t)=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0.5t^2 & 0.5t^2+1 \end{bmatrix}\\ X(t_0)^{-1}=\begin{bmatrix} 0.5t_0^2+1 & -1 \\ -0.5t_0^2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\\ X(t)X(t_0)^{-1}=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0.5(t^2-t_0^2) & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]
