Chapter 1. Linear System

Posted on 2018-11-02 In EE5101 Linear Systems

Input-Output Description

There is a pulse of width \(\Delta\) and height is \(\frac{1}\Delta\) at time \(t_1\). Thus, \[ u(t)=\sum_iu(t_i)\delta_\Delta(t-t_i)\Delta \]

Input Output
\(\delta_\Delta(t-t_i)\) \(g_\Delta(t,t_i)\)
\(\delta_\Delta(t-t_i)u(t_i)\Delta\) \(g_\Delta(t,t_i)u(t_i)\Delta\)
\(\sum_i \delta_\Delta(t-t_i)u(t_i)\Delta\) \(\sum_i g_\Delta(t,t_i)u(t_i)\Delta\)

Therefore, zero-state output is \[ y(t)=\sum_i g_\Delta(t,t_i)u(t_i)\Delta \] If \(\Delta \rightarrow 0\)
\[ y(t)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} g(t,\tau)u(\tau)d\tau \] and if conditional \[ y(t)=\int_{t_0}^{t} g(t,\tau)u(\tau)d\tau \] if a system is time-invariant \[ g(t,\tau)=g(t+T,\tau+T) \] if we choose \(T = -\tau\) \[ y(t)=\int_{t_0}^{t} g(t-\tau)u(\tau)d\tau \]

Linearization

\[ \dot x=Ax+Bu \\ A = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_1} & \dotsm & \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_n} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial f_n}{\partial x_1} & \dotsm & \frac{\partial f_n}{\partial x_n} \end{bmatrix}|x=0,u=0\\ B=\begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial u_1} & \dotsm & \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial u_n} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial f_n}{\partial u_1} & \dotsm & \frac{\partial f_n}{\partial u_n} \end{bmatrix}|x=0,u=0\\ \]

Laplace Transform

\[ F(s)=\int_0^\infty f(t)e^{-st}dt \]

Zero-Pole Cancellation

\[ ^\underrightarrow{V(s)}\space \frac{s-1}{s+1} \space^\underrightarrow{U(s)}\frac{1}{s-1}\space^\underrightarrow{Y(s)} \]

Overall transfer function is \[ \frac{Y(s)}{U(s)}=\frac{1}{s+1} \] If we let \(x_2(t)=y(t)\) , we have \[ \begin{array}{lll} \dot x_2(t) & = & x_2(t)+u(t)\\ U(s) & = & V(s)-\frac{2}{s+1}V(s)\\ X_1(s) & = & -\frac{2}{s+1}V(s)\\ U(s) & = & V(s)+X_1(s)\\ \dot x_1(t)+x_1(t) & = & -2v(t)\\ x_1(t) & = & x_1(0)e^{-t}-2\int_0^te^{-\tau}v(t-\tau)d\tau\\ y(t) & = & x_2(0)e^{t}+\int_0^te^{\tau}u(t-\tau)d\tau \end{array} \] If reverse two system, choose \(x_1(s)\) as U(s) and \(x_2(s)\) as \(-\frac{2}{s+1}x_1(s)\)
